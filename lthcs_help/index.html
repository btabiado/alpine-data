What you’re looking at. LTHCS (Long-Term Hold Confidence Score) ranks ~168 US equities and ~10 large-cap cryptocurrencies on a −100 to +100 scale, refreshed daily. Higher = stronger long-term ownership thesis based on five independent pillars of evidence. Lower = weakening thesis worth a second look.

How to use this page. Jump to the section that matches the number you’re curious about — the table-of-contents on the left is sticky on desktop. Each section answers three questions: what does this number mean?, when should I care?, and what’s the gotcha?

Not investment advice. Everything here is a directional read on committed public data. Backtest results, sizing suggestions, and band labels are educational. Do your own work before risking real money.

Headline number

LTHCS Composite Index #

The big number at the top of the card view (e.g. −20 LTHCS NEUTRAL) is the universe-level composite. It compresses 9 underlying signals into one −100 to +100 read, where 0 = balanced, positive = constructive backdrop for long-term holds, negative = caution backdrop.

What the labels mean

  • BULLISH (≥+40): broad-based strength — pillar averages, band lean, and macro all leaning the same way.
  • CONSTRUCTIVE (+10 to +40): more green than red, but mixed signals.
  • NEUTRAL (−10 to +10): balanced or noisy — no clear directional bias.
  • CAUTIOUS (−40 to −10): more red than green; weakening pillars or risk-off macro.
  • BEARISH (≤−40): broad weakness across pillars and macro.
What it means
Single number summarizing how the whole universe is leaning on the long-term thesis dimension today.
When to care
Big swings vs. the prior day — e.g. +30 → −5 is a real regime shift worth investigating in the components.
Gotcha
"As of YYYY-MM-DD" reflects when the snapshot was computed (23:00 UTC cron). It does not auto-update intraday; refresh after the next daily run.
Component breakdown

What feeds the composite #

Each row in the composite breakdown card is a normalized signal scaled to −100..+100. They average together (with weights from data/lthcs/weights.json) to produce the headline number.

Row by row

  • Band lean — % of the universe in the top three bands (Elite + High + Constructive) minus % in the bottom two (Weakening + Review). Positive = more strong names than weak ones.
  • Adoption pillar avg — mean Adoption-pillar score across all ranked names. Positive = users/holders/network growth tilting up.
  • Institutional pillar avg — mean Institutional score. Positive = 13F filers and insiders adding, not trimming.
  • Financial pillar avg — mean Financial score. Positive = healthy free cash flow, balance sheet, capital returns.
  • Thesis pillar avg — mean Thesis score. Positive = analyst revisions + price-action posture firming. Often shows n/a in V1 (see flags).
  • DES avg — mean Demand-vs-Earnings Strength. Captures whether the stock's run-up is supported by earnings momentum.
  • Macro regime — risk-on vs. risk-off composite from HY OAS + 2s10s + USD. Positive = risk-on backdrop.
  • Insider conviction breadth — % of names with net-positive insider buying over the trailing window, minus the % net-selling.
  • 13F conviction breadth — same idea, but for institutional 13F filings (qtrly).
What it means
Lets you see why the headline moved. If Macro regime tanks while pillar averages hold, the move is macro-driven, not idiosyncratic.
When to care
When components disagree (e.g. macro +30, Adoption −25) — that’s an active divergence worth digging into.
Gotcha
13F breadth lags by a quarter (~45-day filing window). Insider conviction is fresher but bursty around earnings/lockups.
Methodology

The 5 pillars #

Each ticker’s composite score is the weighted average of five independent pillar scores. Every pillar is itself a −100..+100 read and can be missing (n/a) when its source data isn’t available — see data-quality flags.

Adoption
Who actually uses or holds this thing — product traction, user/holder growth, network footprint.
Signals: retail-app downloads, on-chain active addresses (crypto), employment growth, Wikipedia pageview trend.
Institutional
What sophisticated owners are doing — insiders, 13F filers, ETF positioning, options flow.
Signals: Form 4 insider net buys, 13F qtr-over-qtr deltas, ETF AUM trend, put/call posture.
Financial
Can the business fund itself and return capital — cash flow, balance sheet, dividend & buyback policy.
Signals: TTM free cash flow yield, net cash, dividend coverage, buyback authorization remaining.
Thesis
What the market thinks of the story — analyst revisions and the price-action posture.
Signals: EPS revision breadth, price-target deltas, multi-timeframe trend posture. Often n/a in V1 (see flags).
DES
Demand-vs-Earnings Strength — is the run-up supported by earnings momentum or running on multiple expansion alone?
Signals: trailing return vs. trailing EPS growth, sector-relative.
What it means
The composite is only as good as its non-missing pillars. A name with 5/5 pillars present is much more trustworthy than one with 3/5.
When to care
If a pillar drops out (e.g. thesis_unavailable), the composite gets re-weighted across the remaining pillars rather than treating it as zero.
Gotcha
Pillar weights vary by universe (equities vs. crypto have different mixes). Crypto uses on-chain proxies for Adoption and skips DES.
Score ranges

The 6 bands #

Each ticker’s composite gets mapped into one of six color-coded bands. The thresholds are stable across runs (defined in weights.json), so band membership is the cleanest period-over-period comparison.

Elite
≥ +60
All five pillars firmly positive. The strongest read.
High Confidence
+30 to +60
Most pillars positive, maybe one mixed. Solid long-term posture.
Constructive
+10 to +30
More green than red. Watchable but not high-conviction.
Monitor
−10 to +10
Mixed signals — the default neutral zone. Mostly noise.
Weakening
−30 to −10
Multiple pillars softening. Worth a fresh look at the thesis.
Review
≤ −30
Broadly negative read. Re-underwrite or reduce.
What it means
Discrete buckets for easy scanning. The Card view’s "Buy / Hold / Watch" tiles roll these up: Buy = top 3 bands, Hold = Monitor, Watch = bottom 2.
When to care
Band changes matter more than absolute band — "moved from Constructive to Monitor" is a real signal, "still in Monitor" is noise.
Gotcha
A ticker right at a threshold (e.g. +11) can hop bands day-to-day on small drift. Don’t over-read single-day band moves.
Trend over time

Drift values: 1d / 7d / 30d / 90d #

Drift = today’s composite minus the composite N days ago. Shown on every ticker card and on the composite-index card itself. Positive = improving, negative = declining.

  • 1d drift — usually noise. Single new filing or news cycle. Don’t over-react.
  • 7d drift — smoothes out single-day flukes. First reliable trend window.
  • 30d drift — captures a real shift — e.g. a quarter’s worth of insider activity or one earnings cycle.
  • 90d drift — structural read. If 90d drift is strongly negative you’re looking at a multi-month deterioration.
What it means
How fast the score is changing, on multiple horizons. Drift filter chips (Improving / Stable / Declining) on the card view use 30d.
When to care
When 7d and 30d agree on direction — that’s a confirmed move, not a single-day blip.
Gotcha
If the ticker is new to the universe (<90d of history) the longer drift windows show n/a until enough days accumulate.
Top movers strip

Movers leaderboard #

The horizontal strip near the top of the card view ranks tickers by absolute 7d drift — biggest improvers on one side, biggest decliners on the other. Each chip is clickable: tapping opens the ticker’s detail modal.

What it means
The fastest movers in the universe. Useful for "what changed?" attention triage.
When to care
When a holding of yours appears on the decliners side — that’s your prompt to re-read the thesis.
Gotcha
Empty when the daily pipeline hasn’t produced enough history yet (first 7 days after a new universe member). It can also collapse to a quieter look on low-volatility days — not a bug.
Macro overlay

Market regime panel #

Three small gauges — HY OAS, 2s10s, and USD — that summarize the macro backdrop. They feed the Macro Regime component of the composite.

  • HY OAS (High-Yield Option-Adjusted Spread) — how much extra yield investors demand to hold junk bonds. Tight = risk-on. Widening = risk-off.
  • 2s10s — 10-year minus 2-year Treasury yield. Inverted (negative) historically precedes recessions. Steepening = late-cycle.
  • USD — trade-weighted dollar index. Strong USD typically pressures non-US revenue and commodities; weak USD eases EM and exporters.
What it means
Whether the backdrop is friendly or hostile to long-duration risk assets. Affects every ticker the same way.
When to care
When regime goes risk-off but pillar averages stay positive — possible idiosyncratic opportunities. When both go risk-off — broad caution warranted.
Gotcha
FRED is the primary source; if FRED_API_KEY isn’t set the panel falls back to last-known values. See pipeline health for freshness.
Sub-page

Card view (/lthcs/) #

The default landing page. Shows the composite gauge, regime strip, movers strip, and a filterable/sortable grid of ticker cards. Filters and watchlists compose (intersection): selecting both Elite + a watchlist shows only Elite names in that watchlist.

Common workflows

  • Click a band tile to filter to that band.
  • Click the Buy/Hold/Watch summary tile to filter to that rollup.
  • Use the index buttons (DOW / NASDAQ-100 / S&P 100) to narrow the universe.
  • Click a card to open the ticker detail modal — sparkline, pillar bars, drift.
  • Use the Compare tray to side-by-side up to 4 tickers.
Sub-page

Heatmap (/lthcs/heatmap/) #

Sector-grouped heat grid. Each cell is one ticker, sized by some measure of weight (e.g. universe presence) and colored by current band. Lets you spot sector-level tilts at a glance — "Tech is mostly green, Energy is mostly red."

Hover a cell to see the ticker + score. Click to open the detail modal (same modal as the card view).

Sub-page

Table view (/lthcs/table/) #

Bloomberg-style sortable table of the entire universe. Every column is sortable, and the page supports keyboard nav. Use the Export CSV button to pull the current (filtered + sorted) view to your local machine for further analysis.

When to use it
When you want to scan dozens of tickers at once on a single dimension — e.g. sort by 30d drift to surface the biggest macro-independent movers.
Gotcha
Sort applies to the visible filtered set. Clear filters before sorting if you want the universe-wide ranking.
Sub-page

Crypto (/lthcs/crypto/) #

Same five-pillar framework applied to ~10 mature large-cap cryptocurrencies (BTC, ETH, SOL, etc.). The pillar mix is adjusted — Adoption uses on-chain active addresses + developer activity, DES is skipped (no earnings to compare against), and Institutional uses ETF/fund flows where they exist.

What it means
Crypto composite score with the same −100..+100 scale and the same six bands as equities, so you can compare across asset classes.
Gotcha
Crypto data is more permissive of stale-keep than equities (CoinGecko/CryptoCompare are rate-limited free-tier). Check Pipeline Health if a score looks frozen.
Sub-page

Backtest validation (/lthcs/backtest/) #

Answers "does the framework actually predict forward returns?" Two distinct views live on this page — don’t confuse them.

Engine backtest

The current LTHCS scoring engine, run historically. Shows hit rates and decile forward-return spreads — the academic / IC-style validation. This is the real signal-quality measure.

Legacy P&L

An older naive long-only P&L curve preserved for continuity. It mixes survivor bias with weight changes; do not use it to compare against the live engine. Kept on-page only for historical comparison.

When to care
If the engine’s top-decile spread degrades materially over time, that’s a methodology issue — not a market issue.
Gotcha
Backtests don’t include execution costs, slippage, or borrow costs. Treat them as upper bounds on tradable edge.
Sub-page

Pipeline health (/lthcs/health/) #

Operational view. Three sub-pages tell you whether today’s scores are trustworthy:

  • Health — per-source coverage (today + last 30 days), per-pillar data-quality breakdown, last 14 runs.
  • Pipeline — per-cron output age. Confirms each scheduled workflow is actually producing files.
  • Quality — combined verdict from the monthly pillar / distribution / weight audits (runs the 1st of every month).
When to care
Before reading anything else on the dashboard, if you’ve been away for a few days. Green = trust the numbers; amber = mixed; red = a major source is offline.
Gotcha
A red on Reddit subscriber counts is expected (OAuth-blocked). It doesn’t affect the composite materially.
Sub-page

Position sizing (/lthcs/position/) #

Paper-money allocation calculator. Enter a portfolio size + universe filter, get a suggested sizing weighted by composite band: Elite gets the biggest per-name allocation, Review gets zero, Monitor gets a small token. Caps (per-name and per-sector) prevent concentration.

What it means
A worked example of how you might translate LTHCS bands into position sizes. Not a trading instruction.
Gotcha
Paper money only. There’s no broker integration, no rebalance scheduler, no tax-lot tracking. It rounds to whole shares but ignores commissions.
Sub-page

Public data index (/lthcs/public/) #

A discoverable index of the JSON files this site publishes under /data/lthcs/. External tools, notebooks, and downstream integrations can read these endpoints directly — no API key, no rate limit, no auth.

Most consumers want latest_snapshot.json (stable filename, always today’s full universe scores) or manifest.json (this index, machine-readable).

Reference

Data-quality flags glossary #

When a pillar can’t be computed (source down, data stale, API key absent) the scoring engine emits a flag instead of silently zeroing. Flags appear in the per-ticker detail modal and in the pipeline-health source breakdown.

thesis_unavailable
Analyst revision / price-target data wasn’t available. The Thesis pillar is set to n/a for this ticker and the composite is re-weighted across the other four pillars. Common in V1 because the free-tier news/sentiment source returns no usable analyst data.
sec_unavailable
SEC EDGAR fetch failed (rate limit or outage). 13F + Form 4 signals for affected tickers fall back to the last successful snapshot. If multi-day, you’ll see it on the Health page.
crypto_thesis_unavailable
Crypto Thesis pillar can’t be computed (no analyst coverage equivalent for the asset). Same re-weighting behavior as equity thesis_unavailable.
insider_stale
Insider conviction signal is older than the staleness threshold (typically >5 trading days). The pillar still scores, but with a confidence haircut.
13f_lag
13F filing is in the normal ~45-day reporting window between quarter-end and filing deadline. Score uses the prior quarter; not an error.
macro_stalekept
FRED fetch failed; Macro Regime gauges show the last successful values. The composite uses the stale-but-recent macro read.
universe_new
Ticker joined the universe within the last 90 days. Drift windows that exceed the available history show n/a until enough days accumulate.
Background

About the framework #

LTHCS is a free-tier, public-data scoring framework for long-term equity and crypto holdings. It runs daily at 23:00 UTC via a scheduled GitHub Action, commits the snapshot JSON to the repo, and renders it through this static site (no backend, no auth, no rate limits for readers).

The full build spec, methodology, and pillar definitions live on GitHub: PHASE_1_BUILD_SPEC.md and README_LTHCS.md.

Reminder. Nothing on this site is investment advice. LTHCS is an educational research project shipping in the open.

Static help page — reflects the framework as of the last commit. Found something stale or unclear? Open an issue.
